# Onboarding Friction Analysis

## Problem
Users often abandon applications during onboarding, even when core functionality is simple.

## Observations
- Long forms increase drop-off
- Unclear value proposition reduces motivation
- Early errors create negative first impressions

## Common Sources of Friction
- Mandatory sign-up before value exposure
- Excessive permissions
- Lack of progress indicators

## Impact on Users
- Reduced willingness to continue
- Lower trust in the product
- Increased uninstall rates

## Conclusion
Reducing onboarding friction by deferring complexity and clearly communicating value improves early user engagement.

## Open Questions
- How much information is essential at onboarding?
- When should trust-building signals be introduced?
